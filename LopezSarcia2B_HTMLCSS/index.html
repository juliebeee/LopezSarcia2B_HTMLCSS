Geography of Tres Reyes Islands

Island Composition

Tres Reyes Islands can be found in Barangay Pinggan in Gasan, Marinduque. The name of the islands are originally derived from the three Kings of the Nativity: Gaspar, Melchor, and Baltazar. The Tres Reyes Islands, also called the "Three Kings,” is a chain of three islands off the coast of Gasan.

It is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the province of Marinduque. You can reach the islands in more or less half an hour by boat. You can organize a boat trip with one of the resorts along the coastline or with a local fisherman.

The island is an ideal place for picnics and diving in Marinduque. The islets have a long stretch of coral beach and clear blue-green waters which are perfect for swimming and snorkeling. There is an abundance of marine life and coral reefs under the water.

Gaspar Island

Gaspar Island is located in the south west of Marinduque Island. It is the biggest and the largest island of the Tres Reyes Islands. It has a short stretch of coral beach. In 1981, a sunken ship was found in the area over 130-feet under the waters between Gaspar and the mainland. It was one of the Chinese pirate Limahong’s vessels. The find surrendered artifacts and treasures which are worth millions pesos. Some of it are porcelain jars and plates. The Underwater Archeology division of the National Museum recovered various of artifacts from the wreckage. Today, all of the artifacts are now displayed in the museum for exhibit.

Melchor Island

Melchor Island is located near at the mainland of Marinduque among the Tres Reyes islands. There are cottages in the area that are for rent made by the locals. The guests in the area are warned not go too far because there is a strong current in the waters.

Baltazar Island

This is an islands reserve with an excellent dive spot. You can also explore the underwater cave here. Baltazar is one of the most well-known dive areas in the province and among the three islands.

Location and Accessibility

Tres Reyes Islands are positioned near the town of Gasan, along the coastline, making them visible from any point around the town. They lie approximately 30-45 minutes away by boat from the coast. While the islands are secluded and do not have regular transport services, you can easily rent a boat from the mainland for the short trip.

Travelers wishing to visit Marinduque province can reach it by sea. Take a ferry from the city of Lucena to get to the Balanacan port in Mogpog.

Historical Significance

Naming and Heritage

The Tres Reyes Islands are located in Gasan, Marinduque, and are comprised of three islands named Gaspar, Melchor, and Baltazar. These names were derived from the Three Kings of the Nativity, which are associated with the biblical Magi. Each island has its own unique features, and together they hold historical and cultural significance in the region.

Archaeological Discoveries

In the waters surrounding the Tres Reyes Islands, there have been notable archaeological finds that shed light on the region’s history. A Chinese junk, or ancient sailing ship, believed to be over 500 years old, was discovered submerged near the islands southwest of Gasan. This shipwreck has provided valuable insights into international trade and maritime history in the area.

Artifacts recovered from the sunken ship were brought to the National Museum which include porcelain jars, plates, and other relics, showcasing the historical trading ties between China and the Philippines. The Chinese pirate Limahong, who was active during the late 16th century, is also said to have found refuge in the Tres Reyes Islands. This further adds historical and cultural importance to the archipelago.

In conclusion, the Tres Reyes Islands hold significant historical and cultural importance due to their naming heritage and the archaeological discoveries made in the surrounding waters. The recovered artifacts and the stories of the Chinese pirate Limahong provide a glimpse into the rich and colorful history of Marinduque.

Eco-Tourism and Recreation

Beach Activities

The Tres Reyes Islands in Gasan, Marinduque offers visitors a variety of beach activities to enjoy. The islands, named Gaspar, Melchor, and Baltazar, boast of soft white sandy beaches and crystal-clear, azure waters that are perfect for swimming and sunbathing. In addition, the islands provide ample space for family picnics, and beach bumming, allowing guests to take in the natural beauty of the surroundings.

Diving and Snorkeling

The Tres Reyes Islands are a haven for diving and snorkeling enthusiasts, catering to divers of all skill levels. The islands are home to an array of vibrant corals and marine life that thrive beneath the surface. The pristine clear waters surrounding the islands provide excellent underwater visibility and make them a popular destination for snorkelers and casual swimmers alike. The largest islet, Gaspar, offers a coral beach that is particularly suitable for snorkeling.

Nature Exploration

In addition to the water-based activities, the Tres Reyes Islands also offer opportunities for nature exploration, including on-foot hiking and camping opportunities. The lush greenery on the islands makes for scenic hikes and the chance to capture stunning photographs. For those who are more adventurous, there are underwater caves to explore, providing a unique experience for divers.

Apart from the white sandy beaches and underwater caves, these islands are home to a vast array of diverse flora and fauna. Camping enthusiasts will find the Tres Reyes Islands an ideal location, with its tranquil and serene surroundings, perfect for a night under the stars or enjoying the calming sound of the waves.

In summary, the Tres Reyes Islands in Gasan, Marinduque offer a wide range of eco-tourism and recreational activities for visitors to enjoy, such as beach activities, diving, snorkeling, and nature exploration. Embracing the beauty of the islands while preserving their pristine environment is a top priority for the local community.
